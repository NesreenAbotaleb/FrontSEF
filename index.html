Space Cards

Milky Way

The Milky Way is the galaxy that includes Earth, the Sun, and billions of other stars.
It looks like a spinning disk with a bright band of stars when seen from Earth.
Our solar system is located in one of the Milky Way's spiral arms.
Fun Fact: If you look up at the night sky on a clear night, you can see the Milky Way as a fuzzy band of light!

ANDROMEDA

Andromeda is the closest big galaxy to our Milky Way.
It's 2.5 million light-years away from Earth.
It's much bigger than the Milky Way, with about a trillion stars!
Fact: Andromeda is moving towards us, and in 4 billion years, it will collide with the Milky Way!
You can see Andromeda with the naked eye on a clear night!

Caelus

Caelus is a name used for the Roman personification of the sky.
He represents the heavens and the vast expanse above Earth.
Caelus is often compared to the Greek figure Uranus
Fun Fact: His name is related to the Latin word for “sky.”

Black Hole

A black hole is a place in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape.
Horizon: The “point of no return.” Once something crosses it, it can't escape.
Singularity: The center of a black hole where everything is squished into a tiny point.
Accretion Disk: A spinning ring of hot gas and dust around the black hole.
Types of Black Holes:
Stellar: Made when a big star dies.
Supermassive: Giant black holes in the middle of galaxies.

MAGNETAR

A magnetar is a type of neutron star with an incredibly strong magnetic field.
Neutron stars are leftover cores from massive stars that have exploded.
Magnetars have magnetic fields trillions of times stronger than Earth's!
They can create huge bursts of energy, including X-rays and gamma rays.

Black Hole

A quasar is a super bright object in space, even brighter than entire galaxies!
It’s powered by a black hole at its center, pulling in lots of gas and dust.
Quasars give off huge amounts of energy and light, including radio waves and X-rays.
Fun Fact: Quasars are so far away that we’re seeing them as they were billions of years ago!

Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite.
It orbits around Earth and shines at night because it reflects the Sun’s light.
Phases: The Moon goes through different shapes (like full moon, half moon) because of its position relative to Earth and the Sun.
Fun Fact: The Moon has craters from space rocks hitting it, but there’s no atmosphere to blow them away!

RedMoon

A Red Moon happens during a lunar eclipse when Earth comes between the Sun and the Moon.
The Moon looks red because Earth's atmosphere filters sunlight and makes it appear red.
Fun Fact: The Red Moon is sometimes called a "Blood Moon" because of its color.

Sun

The Sun is a giant ball of hot, glowing gas at the center of our solar system.
It gives us light and warmth, making life possible on Earth.
The Sun is a star and is made mostly of hydrogen and helium.
Fun Fact: The Sun’s light takes about 8 minutes to reach Earth!

Bellatrix

Bellatrix is a Bright Star in the constellation Orion.
It’s sometimes called the "Warrior Star" because its name means Female Warrior in Latin.
Bellatrix is much Bigger and Hotter than the Sun!
Fun Fact: It’s one of the three stars that help form Orion’s famous belt.
